WHERE TO GO

islamabad

Islamabad, the capital city of Pakistan is ranked as one of the most beautiful cities in the world.


Punjab

Punjab, is known for its ancient cultural heritage as well as its religious diversity. The lands of Punjab have been home to a number of Sufi Shrines, Hindu Temples and holiest sites of Sikhism. The Indus Valley Civilization once ruled the region. The Gandhara civilization was also quite dominant in the northern region of Punjab at Taxila. Punjab represent a unique mixture of Mughals, Sikhs and Colonial architecture.


Sindh

Sindh is a province of Pakistan. Located in the southeastern region of the country, Sindh is the third-largest province of Pakistan by land area and the second-largest province by population after Punjab. It is bordered by the Pakistani provinces of Balochistan to the west and north-west and Punjab to the north

The province is located in the northwestern region of the country. The total area of the province is 101,741 kmÂ². Khyber Pakhtunkhwa enjoys unique status nationally and internationally due to its rich cultural and natural heritage and its appeal as a destination for adventurers, archaeologists, and explorers. The cultural heritage of the province has prints of Persians, Turks, Greeks, Buddhists, Mughals, and Britishers in the form of artifacts, performing arts, gastronomy, visual arts, languages, literatures, crafts, landscapes, and so on. Tourist destinations with alluring natural beauty are also playing a significant role in providing employment to unskilled people in the most remote locations.


Balochistan

Balochistan, the largest province of Pakistan, spans 347,190 square kilometers, covering 43.6% of the nation's total land area. Despite its vast expanse, the province is the least populated proportionally, with a population of 14,894,402 as per the 2023 census, resulting in a low density per square kilometer. Balochistan is rich in natural resources, boasting abundant minerals such as coal, copper, gold, and natural gas, making it pivotal to Pakistan's mining sector. The province's 760 km coastline along the Arabian Sea, featuring the strategic ports of Gwadar and Pasni, positions it as an essential hub for regional and international trade. Its geography includes the 36,000 sq. km Makran Desert and majestic mountain ranges, enhancing its unique landscape. Known for its cultural diversity, Balochistan is home to ethnic groups like the Baloch, Pashtuns, and Brahuis, who contribute to its rich traditions and heritage. Although facing challenges like economic development and infrastructure gaps, ongoing investments and initiatives aim to harness its potential, paving the way for future growth and prosperity.


Azad Kashmir

Azad Jammu and Kashmir, abbreviated as AJK and colloquially referred to as simply Azad Kashmir, is a region administered by Pakistan as a nominally self-governing entity and constituting the western portion of the larger Kashmir region, which has been the subject of a dispute between India and Pakistan since 1947
